Sudoku

A Python-based Sudoku game that's as much a tribute to classic puzzles. Driven by my passion for both logic puzzles and coding, I embarked on this journey to craft an engaging Sudoku experience from scratch. At its core, the game is a fully Python creation, boasting an interactive graphical interface powered by the CMU Graphics Package.

As you dive into the game, you'll encounter a user-friendly interface that invites you to explore the captivating world of Sudoku. I've put my coding chops to work, ensuring smooth navigation through the grid, intuitive cell selection, and seamless input of answers.

Beyond the thrill of the puzzle, this project allowed me to delve into the art of game development and graphical interfaces. From optimizing code for efficiency to crafting an immersive user experience, every aspect of the game bears my creative touch.

So, whether you're a Sudoku aficionado or a curious coder ready to explore, I invite you to take a seat at the digital puzzle table and enjoy the fruit of my coding journey. Welcome to the world of Python Sudoku – where code meets challenge, and every move unlocks a new piece of the puzzle.

C0 Virtual Machine

As I embarked on my journey through CMU's 15-122 Principles of Imperative Computation course, I encountered a pivotal challenge that would bridge the gap between theoretical understanding and practical implementation: the creation of a C0 virtual machine.

This ambitious project, an essential component of the course, allowed me to dive headfirst into the intricate world of low-level programming. Drawing inspiration from the foundational principles I had acquired, I set out to design and code a virtual machine that could execute programs written in the C0 programming language.

At its core, the C0 virtual machine represents the embodiment of computer architecture and system design. With an insatiable curiosity for the inner workings of machines, I meticulously crafted the architecture, bytecode format, and instruction set, breathing life into the virtual realm where code would come to life.

Throughout the process, I grappled with fundamental concepts of memory management, data representation, and program execution. With each challenge, I delved deeper into the intricacies of stack-based processing, pushing and popping values, and managing the flow of control.

One of the most rewarding aspects of this journey was witnessing the convergence of theory and application. As I wrote and debugged the code for the virtual machine, I gained an unparalleled understanding of how higher-level code translates into machine operations. It was a symphony of logical reasoning and creative problem-solving, harmonizing theory and practice in perfect unison.

The C0 virtual machine also introduced me to the realms of optimization and efficiency. Every decision, from choosing the most optimal data structures to enhancing instruction execution, was guided by a desire to craft a system that was not only functional but also performed with finesse.

Ultimately, my endeavor to create the C0 virtual machine encapsulates the quintessential essence of my academic journey. It is a testament to my dedication to delving beyond the surface, to explore the realms where code and execution converge, and to unlock the mysteries that underlie our digital world.

As I look back on the challenges I overcame, the bugs I hunted, and the countless lines of code I crafted, I am reminded of the powerful connection between theory and practice. The C0 virtual machine stands not only as a tangible testament to my growth as a programmer but also as a symbol of the limitless possibilities that emerge when we venture into the heart of computation.

Automated Lightswitch

This was my final project for 18-096 (Getting Started with Electronics: An Experimental Approach), one of my favorite classes that I had in my freshman year. The class taught us the basics of many electrical components, and equipped me with the skills needed to start complete my final project.

The idea for my final project was simple. I was tired of getting up from my bed to turn off the physical light-switch which controlled the lights in my dorm room. I had a lot of fun planning out the project, and seeing it all come together in the end. The hardest part was probably the coding portion of the project as documentation for the controller I was using was pretty scant, and I had to cobble together code I found online and make them work for my project. This is why if you take a look at the code, it looks atrocious, but it works.

Schedulease

I started learning how to drive in Singapore in 2021, right after the peak of the Covid-19 pandemic. Due to the backlog of hundreds of students who had to stop lessons during the pandemic, it was incredibly difficult to book a driving lesson. The way lessons were conducted were that you had to go onto the website, and look for available slots, and reserve that slot for your lesson.

After enrolling, I went onto the website to book my first lesson, and the next available slot, was 3 months later. I did some math in my head, and I realised that I would probably never learn how to drive at that rate. That’s when I remembered the loophole that a staff member at the driving school told me about while I was registering for lessons. People often cancel their lessons the day before they are scheduled. When they cancel, their lesson slots are put back up into the system, and available for booking. One just has to check at the right time to be able to get their desired lesson. Exploiting this loophole however, would require one to be constantly checking the system, which was impossible as I was working full-time during that time.

Thats when I got the idea to code up a bot that would help me do all this. The problem was that I did not know how to code. So in a week, I taught myself the basics of python, learnt the basics of selenium, and coded up a simple webscraping bot that could help me book lessons on the website. I successfully gained my driver’s license within a month of enrolling in the driving school. Looking back, the code was atrocious, with lots of brute forcing solutions to different bugs. However, it is still something that I am proud of, and ultimately, was the experience that drove me to pursue a degree in computer science.
